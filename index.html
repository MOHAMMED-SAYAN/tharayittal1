About Vengara Tharayittal

A Rich History

Nestled in the verdant landscapes of Kerala's Malappuram district, Vengara is a picturesque village with a rich cultural heritage. The name "Tharayittal" carries historical significance, referring to the traditional gathering place where important village matters were discussed.

With a population of approximately 15,000 people, our community is known for its warm hospitality, cultural diversity, and deep-rooted traditions. The village has evolved through centuries while preserving its unique identity and customs.

Tourist & Visitor Guide

How to Reach Vengara

By Air

Calicut International Airport is the nearest airport, located approximately 35 km from Vengara. Taxis and buses are available from the airport.

By Train

The nearest railway stations are Feroke (20 km) and Calicut (30 km). From there, you can take local buses or taxis to reach Vengara.

By Bus

Kerala State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) and private buses operate regular services to Vengara from major cities in Kerala.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Vengara is between October and March when the weather is pleasant. The monsoon season (June to September) offers lush green landscapes but may limit outdoor activities.

Local Customs

Vengara has a diverse community with rich traditions. Visitors are encouraged to dress modestly when visiting religious places. The locals are friendly and welcoming, often greeting visitors with traditional Kerala hospitality.
